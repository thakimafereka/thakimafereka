History and Significance of Maletsunyane Braai Festival


This expo and event promotes and provide global tourism showcasing through sports activities,culnary skills,hiking and the fun of outdoor camping.These major event acts as a primary source of showcasing and has a positive impact like financial boost for Lesotho’s sports and tourism industry, attracting international participants,investors and media coverage.


Maletsunyane Braai Festival:

  • While primarily cultural, this event at the iconic Maletsunyane Falls includes significant sports tourism elements like hiking and camping and it is Africa's largest and highest-altitude barbecue event, held annually in the highlands of Lesotho. Established in 2016, the festival has grown into a major regional attraction that blends culinary celebrations with high-altitude adventure.The festival takes place at the majestic Maletsunyane Falls in Semonkong, Maseru. The site sits at an elevation of 2,260 meters above sea level.

  • It is held annually over three days during the last weekend of November.The next edition of the festival is scheduled for 27–29 November 2026.It is often called the "Coolest Braai Festival," it brings together thousands of travelers from across Southern Africa (South Africa, Botswana, Eswatini) and beyond for a weekend of camping and grilling

    • Motives and Aims for the Festival


    • Culinary Experience: The heart of the event is the massive open-air grill where a variety of premium meats reflecting local and regional styles are prepared.
    • Adventure Activities: Attendees can participate in thrilling activities at the falls, which hold the Guinness World Record for the longest commercially operated single-drop abseil (192m–204m). Other activities include gorge hiking, horseback riding, and quad biking.
    • Music & Culture: The event features live performances from top local and international DJs and artists, ranging from traditional Basotho music to modern genres. It also serves as a cultural showcase for Basotho traditions, such as the iconic Basotho blankets.
    • Lifestyle & Camping: Most attendees stay in "Tent City," a massive campsite under the stars where visitors bring their own gear or book glamping packages.
    • Stay: While many camp on-site, nearby options include the Semonkong Lodge (known as the "Place of Smoke") and various village homestays for a more authentic cultural experience.
    • Travel: Semonkong is approximately a 4-hour drive from Lesotho’s capital, Maseru, via a tarred road with a perfect view of the mountain kingdom.
